Uma Thurman

Thurman began her professional career as a model, following her mother's example, before she was discovered for the big screen. One of her first known roles was as June Miller in Henry & June opposite Fred Ward . With nominations for an Oscar and a Golden Globe , she got her international breakthrough in 1994 for the role of the self-destructive gangster bride Mia Wallace in Quentin Tarantino's cult film Pulp Fiction . This was followed by less successful leading roles, such as in the vision of the future Gattaca , in the comic film Batman & Robin(both 1997) and in a theatrical version of the British television series Mit Schirm, Charme und Melone (1998) , which was considered by critics and audiences to have had an accident . In 2003, she received a Golden Globe for her role in the TV movie Hysterical Blindness , and made a comeback as a co-writer, again collaborating with Tarantino, as the avenging bride in Kill Bill - Volume 1 and Volume 2 .
